What is SHPE?

SHPE stands for the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers. We are a national organization dedicated toward making the hispanic community reach its fullest potential. Our five pillars include Academic Development, Professional Development, Chapter Developments, Leadership Development and Outreach. There are 10,500 + members at a national level.

How much does membership cost?

The cost of membership for the SHPE UCSD chapter is free. However, there is a $10 fee per year in order to be recognized as a member of SHPE on a national level. This includes, but is not limited to the following benefits:

  • Be eligible to receive scholarships (local and on a national level)
  • Attend SHPE conferences
  • Regional Leadership Conference
  • National Institute Leadership Advancement
  • SHPE National Conference
  • Monthly subscription to the SHPE magazine
  • Exclusive opportunities within SHPE UCSD

When are your General Body Meetings?

Thursday at 6:30pm on even weeks (Week 2, 4, 6, 8, & 10) in the Henry Booker Room (Room 2512) inside Jacobs Hall.

Is it only for Engineers?

Not at all! Any STEM field majors will benefit from our org etc.

I am not Hispanic, can I still come?

Absolutely! We encourage all who are interested to come check out all the great opportunities we have to offer.

How can I get involved?

You can join by coming out to our GBM’s and our socials! If you are interested in outreach or finances, please consider signing up for our Outreach or Finance committees. If you are a freshman/sophomore, we highly encourage you to sign up for our MentorSHPE program in order to get paired with an upperclassmen to guide you along your college experience. Likewise, if you are junior or above and wish to share your advice with someone younger, feel free to sign up as well. We also have project teams, intramural soccer and volleyball teams, and local outreach events at Logan Elementary.

What is PEI?

Professional Evening with Industry is an event dedicated to Diversity. Companies are excited to work with diverse students due to the widespread knowledge that these students have to offer. It is a 3-hour event, consisting of a free dinner alongside recruiters/engineers from 18-20 different companies. The last 2 hours are geared toward further enhancing your professional skills by means of a fairly exclusive career fair.

What is the High School Conference?

It is an all-day event, organized by SHPE. We host about 150 middle/high school students and we bring them on campus to provide them with information about college and STEM fields. We provide workshops focused on STEM, lab tours of research facilities, and also bring in volunteers to inspire and mentor the students, answering any questions they may have along the way.

How do you manage time for school and being part of an org?

It is all about time management. You have to be your own advocate and make sure that studies come first. I had trouble with this at the beginning but realized that through SHPE I am surrounded by my friends dedicated to help me out in school. An org focused on improving my academics has definitely helped me in school.

Isabel Jauregui
Chapter Secretary & Project Coordinator, 2015-2016

How can participating in SHPE benefit me?

SHPE members and the organization as a whole are dedicated toward helping you accomplish your goals. Academic goals. Professional goals. Leadership Goals. Community Goals, etc. We provide the resources needed to improve your overall professionalism by providing resume building workshops or helping you develop an elevator pitch. We also facilitate the process of networking with professionals. SHPE brings in resources to help you learn about grad school, industry, and much more during GBM’s and we create a social atmosphere in order to make you feel at home with your SHPE Familia.

What is your favorite part of being in SHPE?

It helps me accomplish my goals, of any sorts. Whether improve professionally, as a leader, or even my academics. SHPE has always provided me the support and the familia to improve.

Alex Buitimea
Chapter President, 2015-2016
